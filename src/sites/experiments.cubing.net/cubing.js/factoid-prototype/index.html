Factoid 1

Every scrambled Rubik's Cube can be solved in at most 20 moves.

Factoid 2

The first officially recognized world record 22.95 seconds, set by Minh Thai at the World Championship in Hungary in 1982.

Factoid 3

The World Cube Association recognizes 17 official competitive events for the Rubik's Cube and related puzzles.

Factoid 4

The most popular method for solving the Rubik's Cube is to solve "layer-by-layer" starting from one side.

Most speedcubers use an advanced form of this method where they combine steps to reduce the number of decisions they need to make under time pressure.
